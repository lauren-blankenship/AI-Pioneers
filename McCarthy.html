John McCarthy: An AI Pioneer

Explore the life and work of the man who coined "Artificial Intelligence."

Early Life & Education

John McCarthy was born on September 4, 1927. As a teenager, he taught himself college-level math. He earned his B.S. in Mathematics from Caltech in 1948 and his Ph.D. from Princeton University in 1951.

Coined "Artificial Intelligence"

In 1955, McCarthy proposed a summer workshop at Dartmouth College to explore the possibility of creating "thinking machines." He titled the proposal, "A Proposal for the Dartmouth Summer Research Project on Artificial Intelligence," coining the term for the first time.

"The science and engineering of making intelligent machines."

- John McCarthy, 1955

The LISP Programming Language

To support his research in AI, McCarthy developed the LISP (LISt Processor) programming language in 1958. It was designed for symbolic computation and became the preferred language for AI research for decades, influencing many modern languages.

Pioneering Time-Sharing

McCarthy also invented computer time-sharing, a concept that allowed many users to access a single computer simultaneously. This revolutionary idea was a vital precursor to modern interactive computing and cloud services.

Influence on Modern AI

McCarthy's work established the symbolic AI approach, which focuses on formal logic and knowledge representation. This foundation continues to influence areas like knowledge graphs and automated reasoning. He provided the core vision that drives the entire field of AI today.
