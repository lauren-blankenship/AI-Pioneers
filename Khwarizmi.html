Muḥammad ibn Mūsā al-Khwārizmī

The Father of Algebra and Algorithms

A Persian polymath who played a pivotal role in the advancement of mathematics, astronomy, and geography during the Islamic Golden Age. His work is the foundation of modern computer science.

A Scholar of the Islamic Golden Age

Born around 780 CE, al-Khwārizmī was a key scholar at the **House of Wisdom** (Bayt al-Ḥikma) in Baghdad. This renowned institution was a center for translation and research, attracting brilliant minds from across the world.

Revolutionary Mathematical Works

Algebra

His book, "*Kitab al-Jabr wa al-Muqabala*", introduced the formal discipline of algebra. The very word **'algebra'** is derived from the title of this work.

Numerals

He introduced the **Hindu-Arabic numeral system**, including the concept of **zero**, to the Western world, which revolutionized arithmetic and made complex calculations possible.

Astronomy & Geography

He created astronomical tables and revised Ptolemy's work on geography, contributing significantly to cartography and the understanding of the known world.

The Origin of 'Algorithm'

Al-Khwārizmī's systematic, step-by-step approach to solving mathematical problems was so influential that his name, when Latinized as **"Algoritmi,"** became the root of the word **"algorithm."**

The Foundation of Modern Computing

Al-Khwārizmī's logical frameworks laid the groundwork for today's digital world. The concept of an algorithm—a finite sequence of instructions for solving a problem—is the bedrock of all computer programming and, by extension, all forms of Artificial Intelligence.

  • Machine Learning: Algorithms are used to train models on data.
  • Natural Language Processing: Algorithms analyze and understand human language.
  • Computer Vision: Algorithms process and interpret images and videos.
